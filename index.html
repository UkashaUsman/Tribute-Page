Tribute to Quaid-e-Azam
Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah (1876-1948)

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founder of Pakistan, played a pivotal role in the creation of an independent Muslim state in 1947. Known as the "Great Leader," Jinnah's vision, determination, and political acumen were instrumental in the formation of Pakistan.

Born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, Jinnah became a prominent lawyer, a leader of the All India Muslim League, and a symbol of Muslim unity in the Indian subcontinent. His efforts to secure rights for Muslims led to the historic Lahore Resolution in 1940, which ultimately laid the foundation for the creation of Pakistan.

As Pakistan's first Governor-General, Jinnah worked tirelessly to build the newly founded country. He passed away on September 11, 1948, but his legacy continues to inspire millions of people across Pakistan and beyond.
